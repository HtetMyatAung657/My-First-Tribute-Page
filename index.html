Linus Torvalds

American-Finnish software engineer

Here's a time line of Linus Torvalds's early life:

  • 1960-His parents were campus radicals at the University of Helsinki
  • 1969-born in Helsinki, Finland
  • 1988 and 1996-attended the University of Helsinki
  • 1990-Torvalds bought computer science professor Andrew Tanenbaum's book Operating Systems: Design and Implementation, in which Tanenbaum describes MINIX, an educational stripped-down version of Unix.
  • 1981-his interest in computers began with a Commodore VIC-20,
  • 1991-He wrote a Pac-Man clone named Cool Man.
